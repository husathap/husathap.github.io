About Me

I am a PhD student at Dalhousie University under the supervision of Prof. Derek Reilly. I am also concurrently holding a MITACS Accelerate intern position at Ericsson. My specialization is in the areas of cross reality and statistics. Prior to my PhD program, I was a MSc student at the University of Calgary under the supervision of Prof. Wesley Willett. My topic was about computer-assisted language learning. I was an undergraduate student at the University of Toronto. My programs were specialist in computer science, major in cognitive science, and minor in French as a second language. Occassionally, I teach university classes and provide data science services.

I previously worked with the Government of Nova Scotia to analyze online survey data for Windsor/West Hants Together. I also worked with Lenovo as a Research Intern. During my time at the University of Toronto, I also worked as an assistant for TAGLab at the university.
