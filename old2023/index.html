About Me

I am a Thai-Taiwanese-Canadian computer science PhD candidate at Dalhousie University. My current work involves using mixed reality (AR/VR) to create an interactive machine learning system. I was a sessional lecturer at Dalhousie University, and an Ericsson intern. Previously, I was Master's degree student at University of Calgary specializing in computer-assisted language learning. I obtained my Bachelor's degree at University of Toronto in computer science, cognitive science, and French.

PhD Projects

Gander (2018-Now)

Past prototype of Gander in Unity.

Gander is my main PhD project. It is an AR+tablet interface for a multiple linear regression system. This project is partially funded by MITACS and Ericsson, Canada. The work has produced the following publication and patent:

  • Hu, S., Bashbaghi, S., & Reilly, D. (2022). [Patent Pending] Augmented Reality + Tablet Interface for Multiple Linear Regression Model Creation.
  • Hu, S., Malloch, J., & Reilly, D. (2021). A Comparative Evaluation of Techniques for Locating Out-of-View Targets in Virtual Reality. Proceedings of Graphics Interface 2021, 202 - 212. doi: 10.20380/GI2021.32.

Windsor/West Hants Together (2019-2020)

I analyzed the online survey data collected during the merger of Windsor and West Hants, Nova Scotia, Canada.

Dalhousie Classroom Survey (2018-2020)

I surveyd the classrooms at Dalhousie University, and wrote a report on their statuses.

Kalgan: Master's Degree Project

When I was a Master's degree student at University, I developed Kalgan. Kalgan is a web-based video player designed for language learning. I was also an intern at Lenovo, Beijing, China. My Master's degree work is published as a poster paper:

  • Hu, S. & Willet, W. (2018). Kalgan: Video Player for Casual Language Learning. CHI EA '18: Extended Abstracts of the 2018 CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems (LBW053). doi: 10.1145/3170427.3188498.
